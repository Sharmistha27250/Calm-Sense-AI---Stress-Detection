Take a Short Walk
Here's how to make it mindful and calming:
🦶 Feel each step. Concentrate on how it feels to put your foot on the ground and then lift it again.
🔢 Count your steps. Notice the rhythm of your footsteps — left, right, left, right. Let the counting fill your mind.
👁️ Notice small things. The colour of the floor, the texture of the wall, a beam of light. Let your eyes find the little things.
🌬️ Breathe naturally. Don't force it — let your breathing sync with your pace. In through the nose, out through the mouth.
Take at least 2–3 minutes. Walk anywhere — around the room, down the hall, outside.
💧
Put Your Hands in Water
This simple technique activates your senses and breaks the anxiety loop:
🌡️ Start warm. Let warm water run over your hands. Feel the temperature on your fingertips, palms, and the backs of your hands.
❄️ Then switch to cold. Notice how different it feels. Does your breath change? Does your heart rate shift?
🔄 Reverse it. Try cold first, then warm. Does it feel different switching the other way? There's no right answer — just notice.
🤲 Be curious. Does the water feel the same on every part of your hand? Focus only on sensation — nothing else exists right now.
🏃
Move Your Body
Movement releases tension and brings you back into your body. Try any of these:
⭐ Jumping jacks
🦘 Jump up and down
🏃 Jog in place
🤸 Stretch each muscle
👣 Pay attention to sensation. How does the floor feel under your feet? How do your hands move through the air?
🎵 Notice the sounds. Listen to your own movement — your breath, your footsteps, your body in motion.
